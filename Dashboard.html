Why this project: During my internship at US Gypsum Corp., a $1.1B building materials company, I built Power BI dashboards tracking procurement KPIs across an industrial portfolio. USG's core business is drywall, which goes into every permitted building in Chicago. I had deep visibility into internal spend but no view of where construction demand was actually originating in the market. This project uses Chicago's public permit data to answer that question, analyzing where activity is concentrated, which project types are growing, and what the permit mix signals about near-term materials demand.

Key findings
Post-pandemic construction rebound signals sustained materials demand through 2026
Chicago permit volume hit a 6-year high in 2024 at 41,847 permits, up 14.2% from the 2020 trough. Estimated project value grew 22.1% YoY to $3.8B. For a building materials supplier like USG, this translates directly into drywall, ceiling, and partition demand across the city's most active construction corridors.
New construction share recovery is the key materials demand signal
New construction permits climbed to 31.4% of total volume in 2024, up 3.1 pp since 2021. New builds consume 3 to 4 times more raw materials per square foot than renovations. The shift in permit mix is a leading indicator that procurement teams should forecast increased order volume 6 to 9 months forward.
Loop and West Loop represent the highest-value emerging demand corridors
The Loop posted +31.2% YoY permit growth, the strongest of any high-volume market, driven by commercial high-rise restarts. The West Loop followed at +24.3%. Both areas are mixed-use and residential high-rise starts with multi-year materials consumption profiles, giving suppliers with early permit visibility a sourcing and logistics planning advantage.
